Course Overview

8 weeks | 40 hours
3 graduate credits
K-12 Educators

Course Description

This microcredential provides educators with the knowledge and skills to effectively integrate digital technologies into their teaching practice. Participants will explore pedagogical approaches, digital tools, and assessment strategies that enhance student engagement and learning outcomes.

Learning Objectives

  • Analyze and apply digital pedagogical frameworks to teaching practice
  • Design technology-enhanced learning experiences aligned with curriculum standards
  • Evaluate digital tools for their educational value and appropriateness
  • Develop strategies for digital citizenship and online safety
  • Create assessments that measure digital learning outcomes

Course Modules

Module 1: Foundations

Week 1-2

Topics Covered:

  • Digital pedagogy frameworks
  • TPACK model
  • SAMR model
  • Digital literacy standards

Activities:

  • Self-assessment of digital skills
  • Case study analysis
  • Tool exploration journal

Module 2: Design

Week 3-4

Topics Covered:

  • Universal Design for Learning
  • Blended learning models
  • Interactive content creation
  • Accessibility considerations

Activities:

  • Lesson plan redesign
  • Digital resource curation
  • Peer feedback exchange

Module 3: Engagement

Week 5-6

Topics Covered:

  • Student engagement strategies
  • Gamification elements
  • Collaborative online tools
  • Discussion facilitation

Activities:

  • Interactive video creation
  • Discussion board analysis
  • Engagement strategy implementation

Module 4: Assessment

Week 7

Topics Covered:

  • Digital formative assessment
  • Rubric design
  • Data visualization
  • Feedback strategies

Activities:

  • Digital quiz creation
  • Rubric development
  • Assessment portfolio

Module 5: Implementation

Week 8

Topics Covered:

  • Digital citizenship
  • Parent communication
  • Professional learning networks
  • Reflective practice

Activities:

  • Digital citizenship lesson
  • Professional growth plan
  • Capstone project

Learning Outcomes

Knowledge

  • Understand key digital pedagogy frameworks and their application
  • Identify appropriate digital tools for various learning objectives
  • Recognize digital citizenship principles and online safety considerations

Skills

  • Design technology-enhanced learning experiences
  • Facilitate engaging online discussions and collaborations
  • Create and implement digital assessments

Dispositions

  • Value technology as a tool for enhancing learning
  • Demonstrate commitment to continuous professional learning
  • Advocate for equitable access to digital learning opportunities

Applications

  • Implement digital strategies in current teaching practice
  • Share knowledge with colleagues through professional learning communities
  • Reflect on and refine digital teaching practices

Assessment & Certification

Requirements

To earn the Digital Pedagogy Microcredential, participants must successfully complete all course requirements with an average of 80% or higher.

Total Points: 100

Module Activities 60 pts
Discussion Participation 20 pts
Capstone Project 20 pts

Capstone Project

The culminating experience requires participants to design and implement a technology-enhanced learning unit in their classroom, documenting the process and reflecting on outcomes.

Project Components:

  • Unit plan with digital integration
  • Implementation artifacts (student work samples, screenshots, etc.)
  • Reflection on student learning and personal growth
  • Plan for future improvements
